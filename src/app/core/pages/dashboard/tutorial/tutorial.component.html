Getting started: How does it work?

Creating a task

Create a task by clicking the "Add task" button in the top right corner. Besides the usual fields you probably already know from other apps like name, description and tags in Timeliness you set a due date and a workload.
The workload is the amount of time you expect to spend on the task. It is impossible to determine this beforehand with great accuracy, but that's fine, just go for an estimate, as you can always refine this later.

Work units

You will find all of the events, that Timeliness creates or moves in a separate calendar, that Timeliness will create, so they won't pollute your existing calendars.

Work units play a central role in Timeliness. They are not only the main way to track your progress through a tasks life cycle but also a way to portion larger tasks into smaller managable chunks.
After a work unit event ended, Timeliness will ask you to review the work unit and decide if you are done with it. You the mark the work unit as done and it accounts towards the tasks progress or you simply reschedule it.

Calendar features

Having your right in your calendar is not only very helpful regarding visibility, but also very convenient for moving things around.

So what exactly can you do?

  • You can move work units and due dates around as much as you like. They are all synced back to Timeliness.
  • You can delete work units, which reduces the tasks workload or even delete due dates which simply deletes the task with all of its work units.
  • You can move more important events or work unit on top of work units, wait a few seconds, and see them automatically reschedule.
  • You can move work units directly next to each other (or overlap them) as merge them together to one work unit.
